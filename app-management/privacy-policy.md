# Ironbit — Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** 27 June 2026
**Publisher:** Quan Dao
**Contact:** daominhquan1106@gmail.com

Ironbit is an offline-first workout tracker. This policy explains what data the app handles and the
choices you have. It is written to be read, not to bury you in legalese.

> **Beta note:** Ironbit is currently in **beta testing** and distributed to invited testers. Features
> and data practices may still change; we will update this policy and the date above when they do.

## The short version

- Your **training data stays on your device.** Workouts, character, name, bodyweight, and settings are
  stored only in the app's local storage — they are never uploaded to us or anyone else.
- The app collects **anonymous usage analytics** to understand how features are used and improve the
  app, and — **only if you opt in** — **anonymous crash reports** to find and fix bugs.
- You can **turn analytics off** any time in **Settings → Data & Privacy**.
- **No account. No ads. No in-app purchases. We do not sell your data.**

## What stays on your device (never collected)

The following never leave your phone — we never receive them:

- Your workouts (exercises, sets, reps, weights), training history, and programs.
- Your character (name, class, stats, level, items) and profile.
- Your bodyweight and body-metric entries, if you choose to use that feature.
- Your app settings and preferences.

This data lives in the app's local storage and is removed if you uninstall the app or clear its data
in your device settings.

## What we collect

### 1. Anonymous usage analytics — on by default, you can opt out

We use **Google Analytics for Firebase** to collect anonymous, aggregated information about how the app
is used — for example, that a workout was saved, that onboarding was completed, or which screens are
opened. This helps us understand what works and what to improve.

- This data is **not linked to your identity.** It does **not** include your name, your bodyweight, or
  the contents of your workouts.
- Firebase automatically collects standard technical information such as a randomly-generated
  app-instance identifier, device model, operating-system version, and an approximate (country-level)
  region derived from your IP address. See
  [Google's Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy) and
  [How Google uses data](https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites).
- **You can turn this off** any time in **Settings → Data & Privacy → Usage Analytics**. When it is
  off, no usage data is collected.

### 2. Crash reports — off by default, opt in

If you opt in, we use **Sentry** to collect **anonymous crash diagnostics** (the error, a technical
stack trace, device model, and OS version) so we can fix bugs.

- This is **off by default.** It only runs if you enable **Settings → Data & Privacy → Crash Reports**.
- Crash reports do **not** include your name, bodyweight, or workout contents.
- See [Sentry's Privacy Policy](https://sentry.io/privacy/).

## How we use this data

Only to operate and improve Ironbit — to understand feature usage, gauge whether the app stays helpful
over time, and fix crashes. We do **not** sell it, use it for advertising, or share it with anyone
except the service providers named above (Google / Firebase, Sentry), who process it on our behalf.

## Your choices

- **Opt out of analytics:** Settings → Data & Privacy → **Usage Analytics** (off).
- **Crash reports:** off unless you opt in — Settings → Data & Privacy → **Crash Reports**.
- **Delete everything:** uninstalling the app, or clearing its data in your Android settings, removes
  all local data. To request deletion of any anonymous analytics already collected, email us below.

## Data retention

Local data stays on your device until you delete it or uninstall the app. Anonymous analytics and crash
data are retained by our service providers (Google, Sentry) according to their standard retention
settings.

## Children

Ironbit is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your country). We do not
knowingly collect data from children.
